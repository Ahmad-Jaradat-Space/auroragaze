Carrington Event 1859. Reconstructed Dst on the order of -800 to -1760 nT;
Kp at saturation (9). The event followed a white-light flare observed by
Richard Carrington from Redhill on September 1, 1859. CME transit time was
about 17.6 hours, the fastest documented.

Satellite impact: not applicable (no satellites). Telegraph operators
reported sparks from grounded equipment, telegraph keys jamming, and
operators able to send messages with batteries disconnected.

Aurora visibility: aurora seen over Cuba, Hawaii, Colombia, and the Caribbean
in the northern hemisphere. In the southern hemisphere reports include
Queensland (Brisbane and northwards), Honolulu, and Santiago de Chile.

Reference frame: the canonical worst-case scenario in modern engineering risk
analysis. Modern infrastructure has not been tested at this magnitude. Used
as the upper-bound case in NOAA SWPC reference scenarios.

Out-of-scope warning: any briefing claiming a "Carrington-class" event is
imminent without concrete present-day evidence is a hallucination flag.
