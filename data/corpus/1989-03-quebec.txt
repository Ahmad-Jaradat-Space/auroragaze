# date=1989-03-13 kp_peak=9 bz_min=-30 dst_min=-589 source=https://www.swpc.noaa.gov/news/march-1989-event event_type=storm persona=both

March 1989 Hydro-Québec storm. The CME arrived at Earth on March 13 1989.
Kp reached 9. Dst reached -589 nT, the deepest Dst excursion in the modern
record. The geomagnetically induced currents tripped the Hydro-Québec power
grid; the entire province lost power for about nine hours. Multiple
transformers across the North American eastern grid were damaged.

Satellite impact: GOES-7 lost half its solar array output permanently. The
Anik-E1 and Anik-E2 commercial communications satellites suffered
attitude-control failures. Multiple GEO satellites reported surface charging.
SOHO did not exist yet.

Aurora visibility: aurora was photographed in Cuba, the Cayman Islands, and
the Florida Keys in the northern hemisphere. In the southern hemisphere
reports included aurora as far north as Cairns in tropical Queensland; this
is among the lowest-latitude southern aurora sightings in the modern record.

Reference frame: the canonical reference for power-grid GIC damage and for
"low-latitude aurora was not just hyperbole" historical context. The most
extreme Dst excursion since 1957.
