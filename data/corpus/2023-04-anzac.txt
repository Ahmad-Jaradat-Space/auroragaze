# date=2023-04-23 kp_peak=8 bz_min=-32 dst_min=-213 source=https://www.swpc.noaa.gov/news/severe-g4-geomagnetic-storm-watch-23-april-2023 event_type=storm persona=both

April 2023 Anzac Day storm. A halo CME from active region 13283 arrived at
Earth on April 23. Kp reached 8 (G4 severe). Dst reached -213 nT. Bz turned
sharply southward to -32 nT for several hours.

Satellite impact: multiple operators reported anomalies; LEO drag increased
by an estimated 15-25 percent at 400-500 km altitude. No widely-publicised
satellite losses but increased station-keeping reported.

Aurora visibility: substantial southern-hemisphere aurora event. Photographs
reported from Tasmania (very widely), Victoria including Melbourne suburbs,
South Australia (Adelaide and beyond), parts of southern New South Wales,
and the South Island of New Zealand. The auroraaustralis.org.au community
recorded one of its largest event galleries to date.

Reference frame: a strong-but-not-extreme storm. Useful as the modern
reference case for southern Australia aurora at G4 conditions, and as a
calibration point for "what does Kp 8 actually look like at -38°S."
