# date=2024-05-10 kp_peak=9 bz_min=-50 dst_min=-412 source=https://www.swpc.noaa.gov/news/may-2024-storm event_type=storm persona=both

May 2024 Gannon storm. The first G5 (extreme) geomagnetic storm since the
Halloween storms of October 2003. A series of CMEs from active region 13664
arrived at Earth from May 10. The interplanetary magnetic field turned strongly
southward with Bz reaching -50 nT. Solar wind speed exceeded 800 km/s at peak.
Planetary Kp reached 9 on the night of May 10-11 UTC. Dst reached -412 nT.

Satellite impact: SpaceX reported significant atmospheric drag on the Starlink
fleet, requiring extensive station-keeping. Multiple operators reported single
event upsets and surface charging anomalies. GPS positioning errors of up to
several metres were reported. HF communications were degraded across most
sectors for 24 to 48 hours.

Aurora visibility: The southern auroral oval extended unusually equatorward.
Confirmed aurora photographs from as far north as Brisbane (-27.5°) on the
Australian east coast and from northern Queensland reports the next night.
Photographers across Tasmania, Victoria, and South Australia reported
overhead aurora with full red-green-purple structure. New Zealand reports
came from as far north as Auckland. Cloud-cover impact was significant on
the east coast of Australia but inland sites had clear viewing.

Reference frame: this event is the modern analogue for satellite-fleet drag
risk and for "aurora visible from temperate Australia" planning.
