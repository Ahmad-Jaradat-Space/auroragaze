Bureau of Meteorology Australian aurora reporting overview. The Bureau of
Meteorology Space Weather Services issues alerts when the southern auroral
oval is forecast to extend over Australia. Their alert thresholds:
- Aurora alert level 1: Kp 5+, possible aurora from Tasmania.
- Aurora alert level 2: Kp 7+, possible aurora from southern Victoria,
  southern South Australia, and southern Western Australia.
- Aurora alert level 3: Kp 8-9, aurora across most of southern Australia
  and possibly into central Australia.

Best viewing conditions in Australia:
- Dark sky site away from city lights.
- Look toward the south horizon.
- Avoid full-moon nights.
- Check cloud cover via BoM forecasts.

Useful Australian dark-sky locations for aurora viewing:
- Tasmania: Bruny Island, South Arm, Cradle Mountain, Tarkine, Mount Wellington.
- Victoria: Otways, Wilsons Promontory, Cape Schanck, Aireys Inlet.
- South Australia: Fleurieu Peninsula, Kangaroo Island, Yorke Peninsula.
- Western Australia: Albany, Esperance, Margaret River.
- New South Wales: south coast (Eden, Bermagui, Tathra).

Reference frame: this is the Australian operational reference for matching
Kp values to viewing-region expectations.
