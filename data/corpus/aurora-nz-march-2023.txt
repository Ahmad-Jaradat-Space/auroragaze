# date=2023-03-24 location=New Zealand South Island kp_at_observation=8 source=https://www.aurora-service.org/southern-aurora/2023-march/ event_type=aurora_observation persona=aurora

New Zealand aurora event, March 24 2023. Kp 8 conditions during the
storm. Confirmed observations from across the South Island: Lake Tekapo,
Wanaka, Queenstown, Dunedin (-45.87°S), the Catlins, and Stewart Island.
North Island reports came from Wellington, Wairarapa, and Auckland
(-36.85°S), with Auckland observations being naked-eye horizon glow rather
than overhead structure.

Time window: 09:00 to 14:00 UTC, local time 22:00 to 03:00 NZDT.
Cloud cover was variable across the South Island; clear weather over
Otago and Canterbury. Moon was new; ideal conditions.

Reference frame: a strong calibration point at -45 to -37°S. Auckland
horizon-glow at Kp 8 is at the edge of visibility; Stewart Island
overhead aurora at Kp 8 is reliable.
