# date=2023-11-05 location=Hobart, Tasmania kp_at_observation=7 source=https://auroraaustralis.org.au/2023-11-05-tasmania-event/ event_type=aurora_observation persona=aurora

Tasmanian aurora event, November 5 2023. Multiple confirmed observations
across southern Tasmania between 10:00 and 13:00 UTC (21:00 AEDT to 00:00
AEDT next day). Kp 7 sustained through the observation window.

Reported sites: Bruny Island (south Hobart, dark sky), Goat Bluff, South
Arm Peninsula, Howden, and the eastern shore of Hobart. Photographs show
clear pink-red curtain structure with overhead arcs visible from the darker
sites. Naked-eye visibility reported from Bruny Island and South Arm; arcs
were visible looking southward at about 10 to 30 degrees above the horizon.

Cloud cover was variable. Clear in the south, partly cloudy in the north of
Tasmania (Launceston reports were limited by cloud). Moon phase was first
quarter; moonset was before peak observation window.

Reference frame: this event confirms that Kp 7 with clear southern viewing
gives reliable naked-eye aurora from Tasmania at -42.88°S. Useful as a
ground-truth point for the visibility threshold model.
