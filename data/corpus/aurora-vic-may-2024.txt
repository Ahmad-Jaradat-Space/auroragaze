# date=2024-05-11 location=Victoria, Australia kp_at_observation=9 source=https://auroraaustralis.org.au/2024-05-11-mainland-australia/ event_type=aurora_observation persona=aurora

Victoria aurora observations during the May 2024 G5 Gannon storm. Confirmed
photographs from across the state including suburban Melbourne (-37.81°S),
the Mornington Peninsula, the Otways, and central and northern Victoria
(Bendigo, Castlemaine, and as far north as Mildura at -34.2°S).

Time window: 10:00 UTC May 11 through 16:00 UTC. Local time: 20:00 AEST
to 02:00 AEST. Naked-eye visibility was extensive even from suburban areas.
Photographers reported overhead arcs and curtain structure with red, pink,
and green colours. The aurora was bright enough to be photographed with
phone cameras at urban dark-sky scores.

Cloud cover varied. Eastern Victoria was largely clear. Western Victoria
had some cloud impact. Moon was waxing crescent; minimal interference.

Reference frame: the May 2024 storm produced overhead aurora across the
entire state at Kp 9. This is the calibration point for "what Kp 9 looks
like at -37 to -34°S." Confirms that during G5 conditions the auroral oval
extends well into mainland Australia.
