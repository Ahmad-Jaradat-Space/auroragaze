Akasofu auroral-oval boundary table (reference).

The classical empirical mapping from Kp to the equatorward boundary of
the auroral oval, in geomagnetic latitude (degrees, southern hemisphere
expressed as positive numbers):

| Kp | equatorward boundary (°S geomagnetic) |
|----|---------------------------------------|
| 0  | ~67                                   |
| 2  | ~64                                   |
| 4  | ~58                                   |
| 5  | ~56                                   |
| 6  | ~54                                   |
| 7  | ~52                                   |
| 8  | ~50                                   |
| 9  | ~48                                   |

Geographic-vs-magnetic-latitude offset for Australian/NZ longitudes:
add ~2-4° equatorward (geographic) to convert from geomagnetic. NOAA
SWPC and the Bureau of Meteorology use slightly different conversions;
AuroraGaze's `assess_visibility` table uses a calibrated lookup against
recent observation reports rather than this academic table directly.

Reference frame: this is the canonical Akasofu/Chapman/Meng formulation
that underlies most modern visibility tables. Useful for explanatory
text in briefings.
