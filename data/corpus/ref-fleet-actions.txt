Operational actions for satellite operators during geomagnetic storms.

Standard menu of decisions during a forecast or in-progress storm:

1. Safe-mode the bus: reorient to minimise drag (LEO) or reduce surface
   charging risk (GEO). Used during G3+ storms for sensitive payloads.
2. Defer maneuvers: planned orbit raises, station-keeping burns, or
   formation flying maneuvers can be delayed to avoid pointing changes
   during high-density periods.
3. Raise the orbit: pre-storm proactive altitude increase for very low
   parking orbits to reduce drag exposure. Generally only useful with
   advance warning of 24 hours plus.
4. Disable non-essential payloads: reduce power draw and thermal
   variability while protective settings are active.
5. Increase telemetry cadence: prepare for anomaly detection and faster
   investigation if upsets occur.
6. Notify customers: scheduled downtime windows, expected service quality
   changes (e.g. GPS positioning errors), and contact paths.

Per-orbit-class indicative thresholds:
- LEO below 400 km: action threshold G2 (Kp 6) or higher.
- LEO 400-800 km: action threshold G3 (Kp 7) or higher.
- MEO/GPS: surface-charging threshold G3 plus high-energy electron flux
  enhancement; check GOES SEM data.
- GEO: surface charging G3+; deep-dielectric charging on multi-day events.

Reference frame: a briefing for satellite operators should match the
recommended action to the specific orbit and mission profile, citing the
storm condition that triggered the recommendation.
