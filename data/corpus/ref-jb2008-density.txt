JB2008 atmospheric density model (reference).

JB2008 (Jacchia-Bowman 2008) is the operational atmospheric-density
model used by US Space Force's 18 SDS for orbit determination and
prediction at LEO altitudes. It supersedes JB2006 and the earlier
Jacchia-71 model.

Inputs:
- F10.7 (10.7 cm solar radio flux) — proxy for EUV heating, daily.
- Geomagnetic indices (Kp/ap, Dst) — drive storm-time perturbations.
- Solar EUV proxy (S10, M10, Y10) — capture different EUV bands.

Storm response: density at LEO altitudes increases roughly with
ap-integrated heating; for the same Kp peak a storm with a long
recovery (sustained ap) will deposit more energy than an impulsive
peak. This is why the Feb 2022 Starlink case (Kp 5) caused more
operational damage than some Kp 6 storms — the timing relative to a
210 km parking orbit was the key factor.

Density-fraction increases as a function of Kp at indicative LEO
altitudes are tabulated in `ref-drag-table.txt` for operational use.

Reference frame: the engineering authority for "what does drag do"
quantitatively. The JB2008 reference is the underlying source for the
empirical drag table AuroraGaze uses in `compute_drag_delta()`.
