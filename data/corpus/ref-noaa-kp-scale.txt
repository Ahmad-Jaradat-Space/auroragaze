# date=2024-12-01 reference=true source=https://www.swpc.noaa.gov/noaa-scales-explanation event_type=reference persona=both

NOAA G-scale and Kp index reference. The Kp index is a quasi-logarithmic
3-hour planetary index from 0 to 9. NOAA categorises geomagnetic storms
on a G-scale derived from Kp:

- G1 minor: Kp 5. Power systems weak fluctuations; spacecraft minor impact;
  aurora may be visible at high latitudes (Tasmania in Australia).
- G2 moderate: Kp 6. Power systems voltage alarms possible; spacecraft
  drag may extend onto LEO satellites. Aurora visible from southern
  Victoria, parts of South Australia.
- G3 strong: Kp 7. Voltage corrections required on power systems; surface
  charging on satellites. Aurora visible from much of southern Australia.
- G4 severe: Kp 8. Voltage instability and protective system trips
  possible; satellite tracking issues. Aurora across most of southern
  Australia and into mainland NZ.
- G5 extreme: Kp 9. Power grid widespread voltage problems possible;
  satellite operations severely affected. Aurora possible into central
  Australia and as far north as central Queensland.

Reference frame: this is the canonical NOAA-published mapping of Kp to
storm impact level. Briefings should reference these categories explicitly.
