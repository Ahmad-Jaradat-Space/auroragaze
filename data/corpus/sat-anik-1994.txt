January 1994 Anik-E1 and Anik-E2 anomalies. A G3 geomagnetic storm
combined with sustained energetic-electron flux drove differential
charging on Telesat Canada's Anik-E1 (lost momentum-wheel control on
20 January) and Anik-E2 (lost both primary and backup attitude-control
units within hours, knocking out CBC and other Canadian broadcasts for
several months while ground-control workarounds were developed).

Anomaly mode: deep-dielectric charging on insulating panels followed
by arc discharge into avionics. Both buses were old (launched 1991/
1991) and the differential-charging hardening was below modern spec.

Reference frame: the canonical case for "sustained MeV-electron flux
during a moderate storm caused two GEO satellite outages." Useful
analogue for any GEO operator brief during multi-day storm conditions.
