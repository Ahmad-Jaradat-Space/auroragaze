# date=2010-04-05 kp_peak=7 bz_min=-18 source=https://www.swpc.noaa.gov/news/galaxy-15-event event_type=storm persona=satellite

April 2010 Galaxy 15 anomaly. Intelsat's Galaxy 15 GEO comms satellite
lost contact with ground control on April 5 during a G3 geomagnetic
storm and continued operating its transponders without commands —
becoming a "zombie satellite" that drifted east through the GEO arc
for several months interfering with neighbouring spacecraft.

Anomaly mode: surface charging caused a failure in the Earth-sensor
and command-receiver path; the spacecraft computer continued executing
its last-loaded command sequence. Recovery required waiting for the
batteries to deplete and a controlled reset months later.

Reference frame: the operationally most-cited GEO surface-charging
event of the 2010s; useful analogue for any GEO operator briefing
where command-and-control vulnerability under sustained MeV-electron
flux is a concern.
