March 1989 GPS Block IIA upset event. During the same storm that
collapsed the Hydro-Québec grid, the GPS Block IIA constellation
suffered a sharp rise in single-event upset rates. Multiple satellites
saw memory-bit-flip rates 10× nominal during the storm's main phase.
No satellites were lost, but positioning solutions degraded over a
24-hour period; civilian GPS users saw errors of tens of metres.

Anomaly mode: high-energy proton flux at MEO altitudes (~20 200 km),
particularly during the storm-time particle-injection phase. Block-IIA
hardening was post-Vela but pre-Block-IIR; ECC and watchdog timers
contained the upsets but did not eliminate the positioning errors.

Reference frame: the canonical MEO/GNSS upset analogue. Useful for any
operator brief on GPS, Galileo, GLONASS, or BeiDou under G4+ conditions.
