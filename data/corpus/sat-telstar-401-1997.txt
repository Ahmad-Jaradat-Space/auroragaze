January 1997 Telstar 401 loss. AT&T's Telstar 401 GEO comms satellite
suffered a complete failure during a moderate (G2) geomagnetic storm.
The proximate cause was a power-supply electrical-system failure
attributed to electrostatic discharge from differential surface charging.

The storm was small in magnitude but the satellite was old and the
spacecraft's surface materials had degraded over its operational life.
The loss took down major US east-coast broadcast and data services for
several days; replacement coverage was patched together from Galaxy
satellites.

Reference frame: a "small storm, expensive outage" case showing that
end-of-life satellite vulnerability is the operationally relevant
factor, not just storm magnitude. Useful for any aging-fleet operator
brief.
